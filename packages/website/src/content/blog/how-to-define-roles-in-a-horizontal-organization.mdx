In a horizontal organization, [defining roles](https://www.rolebase.io/plateforme/roles) is essential to avoid confusion and improve collaboration. Here are the key takeaways:

- **Role clarity**: Each role should have a clear name, a specific purpose, a defined scope, and well-established responsibilities.

- **Setting boundaries**: Define authority levels, communication protocols, and work schedules to balance autonomy and accountability.

- **Flexibility and review**: Adjust roles regularly based on team needs and project requirements.

- **Useful tools**: Use methods like the [RACI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Responsibility_assignment_matrix) matrix to clarify responsibilities and tools like [Rolebase](https://rolebase.io/) to visualize roles.

- **Shared decision-making**: Adopt collaborative processes to involve teams while designating a single decision-maker when needed.

### Benefits

- [Fewer unnecessary meetings](https://www.rolebase.io/plateforme/optimisation-des-temps-de-reunions), faster decisions.

- Better collaboration, fewer internal conflicts.

- More autonomous and efficient teams.

### Real-world example

[Spotify](https://newsroom.spotify.com/company-info/) adjusted the roles of its marketing team in 2023, reducing the bounce rate from 12.3% to 2.1% in 60 days, generating $2.3 million.

**In summary:** A horizontal organization succeeds through well-defined roles, clear communication, and continuous adaptability.

## Core Principles for Defining Roles

### Aligning Roles with Company Goals

Make sure roles align with the company's goals and values. Each role should include a **clear name**, a **specific purpose**, a **defined scope**, and **well-established responsibilities**. The RACI matrix is an excellent tool for organizing these responsibilities and simplifying team decision-making. This creates a structured framework that provides both autonomy and clarity for each role.

### Establishing Autonomy Boundaries

Setting precise boundaries is essential to balance individual freedom and collective accountability. Here are some key aspects to consider:

| Aspect | Description | Benefit |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Work hours | Define specific schedules (e.g., "do not disturb" mode) | Reduced burnout |
| Decision-making | Specify authority levels for each role | Improved efficiency |
| Communication | Establish clear communication protocols | Better collaboration |

These boundaries also help protect individuals from professional burnout.

### Adjusting Roles for Greater Flexibility

In March 2023, Spotify's marketing team reorganized its roles to integrate an email verification API. The result? A reduction in bounce rate from 12.3% to 2.1% in just 60 days, generating $2.3 million. This kind of review shows how targeted adjustments can balance stability and flexibility.

To maintain this flexibility:

- Schedule **regular role reviews**.

- Encourage team members to **take initiative**.

- **Communicate changes** to roles clearly.

## Steps to Map and Assign Roles

Once roles are clearly defined, these practical steps help assign them with precision and efficiency within the organization.

### Analyzing Work Patterns

Start by examining work patterns to better understand the roles. The RACI matrix can help clarify responsibilities.

Here are some key elements to analyze:

| Aspect to Analyze | Objective | Evaluation Method |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Information flow | Identify bottlenecks | Process mapping |
| Decision points | Clarify responsibilities | Decision analysis |
| Team interactions | Spot collaboration zones | Direct observation |

These analyses lay the groundwork for the next step: matching skills to roles.

### Matching Skills to Roles

A tool like the "Skills Cloud" can facilitate this step by highlighting the specific skills needed for each role.

> "The more specific your job title, the more your credibility in areas outside of it will erode." - Leif Abraham

To succeed in this matching process, consider:

- Building a **dynamic inventory** of available skills within the team.

- Encouraging **cross-functional development** to broaden team members' capabilities.

- Aligning **projects** with members' **personal interests** for better engagement.

Once skills are identified, specialized tools can help organize and visualize these assignments.

## Methods for Defining Tasks and Decisions

These approaches complement the previous strategies by specifying tasks and decisions in a horizontal organization.

### Writing Role Descriptions as a Team

Writing role descriptions as a team ensures clarity and engagement.

| Aspect | Description | Objective |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Authority | Set authority levels | Clarify autonomy |
| Collaboration | Define collaboration modes | Ensure coordination |
| Responsibilities | Detail tasks | Establish expectations |

> "Holacracy incites us to *think* differently about how authority and accountability flow between roles." – Olivier Compagne, HolacracyOne

To write effective descriptions:

- Use precise terms like *support*, *assist*, or *advise*.

- Clarify collaboration modes.

- Define well-delineated areas of action.

These descriptions serve as a foundation for structuring self-managed teams.

### Creating Self-Managed Teams

The example of [Buurtzorg](https://www.buurtzorg.com/), a Dutch healthcare organization, illustrates the success of self-managed teams. This model relies on autonomous teams of nurses who manage all aspects of care.

To set up a self-managed team:

1. **Develop a team handbook**

  This document describes processes, decision-making methods, and resolution protocols.

2. **[Distribute responsibilities](https://www.rolebase.io/plateforme/responsabilites-des-equipes)**

  Identify necessary activities and group them into roles aligned with skills and motivations.

3. **Establish a work rhythm**

  Set a cadence for:

- [Team meetings](https://www.rolebase.io/plateforme/reunions-dequipe)

- Feedback sessions

- Goal reviews

### Making Decisions as a Team

Once roles are defined and teams are structured, it is crucial to clarify the decision-making process. A "republic" approach, where members contribute but a representative makes the final decision, can be particularly effective.

To improve this process:

- Assign a single decision-maker for each decision.

- Involve people directly affected by the issue.

- Set clear deadlines for each decision.

For complex decisions, start with less critical choices. This helps develop good practices, builds trust within the team, and gradually refines the process.

## Keeping Roles Effective

### Team Feedback Systems

The "Triple A" method (Appreciation, Amplification, Adjustment) is useful for ensuring roles remain relevant.

Here is how to structure an effective feedback system:

| Phase | Objective | Key Actions |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Preparation | Define the framework | Establish evaluation criteria |
| Training | Develop skills | Train on giving constructive feedback |
| Follow-up | Ensure progress | Organize regular check-ins |

> "A role does not belong to a person but rather to the team, the division, or the organization. You do not own a role, you take care of it for a time. Someone took care of it before you, and someone will take care of it after you." - Samantha Slade

These feedback mechanisms allow roles to adapt to evolving needs.

### Role Evolution and Learning

High-performing companies invest in the continuous development of technical, social, and emotional skills.

To foster this evolution:

- Schedule monthly mini check-ins to evaluate assigned roles.

- Set up a buddy system to facilitate the transfer of responsibilities.

- Document learnings and effective methods.

Managers play a key role in this process and must master skills like communication, organization, and problem-solving. These efforts help support changes and quickly resolve potential blockers.

### Resolving Role Issues

Managing role-related conflicts requires a collaborative and transparent approach. Teams must be equipped to identify and resolve issues effectively.

To achieve this:

1. **Early identification**

  Monitor tensions through warning indicators. Tools like Rolebase can help track role evolution and spot overlaps.

2. **Collaborative resolution**

  Foster a culture where employees see themselves as partners. Practice active listening and seek solutions that benefit everyone.

3. **Continuous adjustment**

  Integrate regular evaluations to align roles with changing needs. Strengthening teams after a change increases the chances of project success.

By integrating these practices, organizations can resolve conflicts and maintain roles that are aligned with their goals.

## Conclusion: Building Clear Team Roles

### Key Takeaways

Defining precise roles in a horizontal organization requires a structured and collaborative approach. Here are the main elements:

| Aspect | Objective | Implementation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Communication** | Ensure transparency | Regular meetings and digital collaboration tools |
| **Responsibilities** | Clarify expectations | Distribution based on skills and motivation |
| **Decision-making** | Foster autonomy | Shared decision-making processes |
| **Development** | Encourage growth | Continuous training and constructive feedback |

These pillars, discussed throughout this article, lay the groundwork for regularly adjusting roles and responsibilities.

### The Importance of Continuous Adjustments

To ensure roles remain effective, regular review is essential. Successful horizontal organizations focus on:

- Frequent analysis of responsibilities to make sure they remain suited to current needs.

- Revising roles based on changes in projects or priorities.

- Acquiring new skills to support professional development.

The key lies in a flexible and evolutionary approach. Teams should be encouraged to express their needs and suggest adjustments when necessary. This improves collective performance while cultivating a stimulating and enriching work environment.

Rolebase, for example, illustrates this ability to combine agility and structure. These ongoing adjustments embody the spirit of evolution and autonomy that characterizes horizontal organizations.
