**Combining continuous improvement with horizontal management can transform your company's performance. Here's how:**

1. **Decentralize decisions**: Give teams more autonomy to speed up processes.

2. **Encourage feedback**: Set up regular and transparent systems to improve practices.

3. **Set clear objectives**: Define measurable priorities to track progress.

4. **Celebrate successes**: Strengthen collective motivation by recognizing efforts.

5. **Use collaborative tools**: Simplify role and task management with the right digital solutions.

**Key to success**: Combine autonomy, collaboration, and effective tools to embed a culture of continuous improvement within horizontal management.

## Key Principles for Integration

### Leaders as Team Guides

In a horizontal management approach, leaders act as facilitators rather than controllers. Their primary mission is to help teams gain autonomy and improve continuously.

To achieve this, managers should:

- **Facilitate decision-making** by defining a clear framework and precise objectives.

- **Encourage experimentation** by providing an environment where mistakes are accepted and seen as learning opportunities.

- **Strengthen skills** through coaching and ongoing training.

This guiding role relies on a constant flow of feedback, which is essential for sustaining a continuous improvement dynamic.

### Building Effective Feedback Systems

Well-designed feedback systems are essential to support continuous improvement. These systems should meet several key criteria:

| Characteristic | Description | Main Benefit |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Regular | Weekly team meetings | Rapid problem detection |
| Structured | Standardized feedback processes | Consistent and comparable evaluations |
| Transparent | Openly shared results | Trust building |
| Constructive | Solution-oriented | Progress engagement |

Digital collaborative tools simplify the centralization and tracking of feedback while promoting knowledge sharing across teams.

For this feedback to bear fruit, teams must take ownership of the results and turn them into concrete actions.

### Team Accountability and Results

To strengthen collective engagement, several steps are necessary:

1. **Define shared objectives**

Involving teams in setting objectives strengthens their engagement and understanding of priorities.

2. **Establish performance indicators**

Clear and accessible indicators, updated regularly, help track progress and identify areas for improvement.

3. **Celebrate successes**

Recognizing collective achievements reinforces cohesion and encourages ongoing effort. Some best practices include:

- Sharing successes during team meetings.

- Highlighting both individual and collective contributions.

- Using positive feedback to enrich shared practices.

## Methods for Lasting Success

With the foundations in place, apply proven approaches to maintain a continuous improvement dynamic.

### [Kaizen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kaizen) and Lean Methods

The Japanese Kaizen method, centered on continuous improvement, adapts well to horizontal management. It favors regular, small-scale progress over major transformations.

Here's how to apply Kaizen effectively:

1. **Process standardization** Start by documenting existing processes. Each team should analyze its workflows to identify improvement opportunities.

2. **[PDCA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PDCA) cycles** Use the Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) cycle to structure improvements:

- **Plan**: Set precise objectives for improvements.

- **Do**: Test changes on a small scale.

- **Check**: Evaluate the results obtained.

- **Act**: Integrate best practices into standard processes.

This approach allows teams to cultivate a continuous improvement dynamic, which is essential in a horizontal management framework.

- **Waste reduction** Identify and eliminate the 7 common types of waste in management:

| Type of Waste | Example in Horizontal Management |
| --- | --- |
| Overproduction | Unnecessary meetings |
| Waiting | Delays in approvals |
| Transport | Repetitive information exchanges |
| Over-processing | Unnecessary reports |
| Inventory | Accumulating pending tasks |
| Motion | Difficulty navigating between tools |
| Defects | Communication issues |

## Tracking Progress and Maintenance

Tracking, evaluating, and adjusting processes are essential for maintaining a continuous improvement dynamic in horizontal management.

### Analyzing Progress with Data

To make progress, precise performance tracking is needed. Teams should monitor key performance indicators (KPIs) suited to their context:

| Indicator Type | Indicators | Monitoring Frequency |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Productivity | Cycle time, completion rate | Weekly |
| Quality | Error rate, customer satisfaction | Monthly |
| Engagement | Meeting participation, improvement suggestions | Biweekly |
| Collaboration | Response time, problem resolution | Monthly |

Rolebase provides customizable dashboards to visualize data in real time, spot areas for improvement, and act quickly. Once the data is analyzed, it is equally important to highlight successes.

### Highlighting Team Successes

Recognizing achievements boosts motivation and engagement. Here are some ways to showcase progress:

1. **Regular celebrations**

Organize monthly meetings to share results. Highlight key data and positive feedback from stakeholders.

2. **Success archive**

Create a knowledge base where each successful initiative is documented with concrete results. This serves as a guide and inspiration for future improvements.

### Frequent Process Adjustments

Once progress is tracked and celebrated, it is important to update processes regularly:

- Schedule quarterly reviews to evaluate results.

- Organize collaborative workshops to identify improvement opportunities.

- Test and adjust processes through iteration cycles.
