Horizontal management transforms organizations by removing traditional hierarchical levels to foster autonomy, collaboration, and better communication. Here are the key takeaways:

- **Simplified hierarchy**: A single management level for decentralized decision-making.

- **Increased collaboration**: Teams work spontaneously and cross-functionally.

- **Key benefits**: Greater responsiveness, creativity, and employee engagement.

- **Challenges**: Resistance to change and the need for team support.

### Quick Action Plan

1. **Analyze your company culture**: Identify current strengths and weaknesses.

2. **Adopt collaborative tools**: Use software like [Rolebase](https://rolebase.io/) or [Taiga](https://taiga.io/).

3. **Train your teams**: Develop skills in communication and collaborative leadership.

4. **Track progress**: Measure engagement, tool adoption, and decision-making effectiveness.

| **Aspect** | **Vertical Structure** | **Horizontal Structure** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Hierarchy | Multiple levels | Single level |
| Communication | Slow and indirect | Direct and fluid |
| Decision-making | Centralized | Decentralized |
| Collaboration | Planned | Spontaneous and cross-functional |

**Next steps**: Assess your organization, train your teams, and implement the right tools to facilitate the transition.

## Planning the Transition

### Analyzing Company Culture

Before starting the transition, take a close look at the existing culture. Companies with a well-established organizational structure are 30 times more receptive to change and 5.3 times more likely to be perceived as attractive workplaces.

To do this, evaluate your culture along three main dimensions:

| Assessment Area | Key Indicators | Measurement Methods |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Organizational Performance | Productivity, absenteeism, tenure | HR dashboards |
| Employee Engagement | eNPS, participation rate | Satisfaction surveys |
| Values Alignment | Observed behaviors, external feedback | 360° evaluations, [Glassdoor](https://www.glassdoor.com/Reviews/index.htm) reviews |

> "Culture is not an initiative. Culture is the enabler of all initiatives." - Larry Senn

Companies with engaged employees experience revenue growth 2.5 times higher over a seven-year period. Once this assessment is complete, rally your teams to carry out the transformation.

### Strengthening Team Alignment

Team alignment is a critical step in making the transition successful. Rolebase offers a platform dedicated to visualizing and managing the new organizational structure, simplifying the gradual shift toward a horizontal model.

Here are some essential actions to take:

- **Set clear objectives**:

- Promote employee well-being

- Boost performance

- Improve brand image

- **Develop a concrete action plan**:

- Train managers

- Introduce collaborative tools

- Redesign workspaces

- Regularly monitor key indicators

- **Ensure open communication**:

  90% of employees in a high-performing culture report having trust in their leadership.

> "Horizontal alignment is not just a strategy; it is becoming a fundamental aspect of organizational DNA, driving collective achievement and growth." - Yanzhen Li

To support your teams through this change, provide them with the necessary resources and ongoing support. Organize regular training sessions and interactive workshops to encourage adoption of new practices.

## Fundamental Management Changes

### Role Distribution

Transitioning to horizontal management means redefining responsibilities and eliminating intermediate levels to encourage direct communication.

| Aspect | Before the Transition | After the Transition |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Decision-making | Centralized | Decentralized by team |
| Manager's role | Directive | Mentor and facilitator |
| Communication | Top-down | Multidirectional |
| Responsibilities | Fixed | Shared and flexible |

These adjustments enable effective decentralization of decisions, which is essential in this model.

### Decision-Making Methods

Decentralization is a cornerstone of horizontal management. According to research, organizations with advanced organizational design are **2.3 times more financially performant**.

Here is a typical breakdown for improving the decision-making process:

- **90%** of decisions made individually (clear mandate and reversible decisions)

- **9%** by group consent

- **1%** through advisory process for strategic choices

> "Move decision-making authority to the information and enable those that feel a sense of urgency to take the initiative." - Manuel Küblböck

For this to work, clear communication rules are essential.

### Communication Standards

In a horizontal model, communication must be transparent and fluid. Take Google as an example: the company fosters open exchanges through collaborative platforms and regular meetings like "TGIF," where employees interact directly with leadership.

To establish effective communication:

- **Define channels**

  Create rules for each channel, covering:

- Response times

- Schedules and protocols

- **Design the workspace**

  Facilitate spontaneous interactions with:

- Informal meeting areas

- Open spaces

- Collaborative gathering points

- **Train and support**

  Offer regular training to:

- Master collaborative tools

- Adopt horizontal communication techniques

- Run participatory meetings

> "The general trust among employees can build up in an organization if every person is committed to engaging in effective communication." - Greek study

Tools like those from Rolebase, with dynamic organizational charts and role management features, can simplify this transition to a horizontal structure.

## Change Management

### Staff Training

Training plays a key role in a successful transition. Managers must acquire new skills, shifting from a decision-maker role to that of a coach.

To make this training effective, here are some approaches:

**Programs for Managers**:

- Developing collaborative leadership

- Personalized coaching

- Using the new tools

**Team Training**:

- Mastering collaborative work tools

- Improving communication skills

- Techniques for effective collective decision-making

These efforts help teams adapt better and follow the transition in a structured way.

### Tracking Progress

Rigorous tracking relies on both qualitative and quantitative data.

| Indicator | Measurement Method | Frequency |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Team engagement | Anonymous surveys | Monthly |
| Decision effectiveness | Average decision time | Weekly |
| Tool adoption | Usage rate | Daily |
| Employee satisfaction | Individual interviews | Quarterly |

With only 11% of organizations reaching advanced maturity, structuring the tracking process is essential for continuously adjusting the strategy. Key points to remember:

- Set precise and measurable objectives

- Regularly share progress with teams

- Adjust approaches based on feedback

These indicators help identify obstacles and correct them quickly.

### Process Improvement

In a horizontal structure, continuous improvement is essential. The highest-performing companies are 30 times more responsive to change thanks to this approach.

**How to Proceed**:

- Identify blockers and propose solutions as a team

- Test these solutions on pilot projects

- Measure results and adjust as needed

Achieving quick wins motivates teams and facilitates lasting adoption of horizontal management. These small victories reinforce the momentum of change over the long term.
