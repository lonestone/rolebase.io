**Horizontal management** is a model that simplifies hierarchies, fosters autonomy, and improves team productivity. It relies on decentralized decision-making, direct communication, and spontaneous collaboration. Unlike vertical management, it speeds up decisions, stimulates innovation, and strengthens employee engagement.

### Key takeaways:

- **Benefits**: Simplified communication, faster decisions, increased innovation, stronger engagement.

- **Challenges**: Unclear roles, alignment difficulties, complex management in large organizations.

- **Examples**: [Valve Corporation](https://www.valvesoftware.com/) (full autonomy), [Morning Star](https://www.corporate-rebels.com/blog/morning-star-pioneering-self-management-in-manufacturing) (decisions without managers), [W.L. Gore](https://www.gore.com/) (structure without strict hierarchy).

### Quick comparison:

| Criterion | Horizontal Management | Vertical Management |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Decision-making** | Decentralized and autonomous | Centralized |
| **Communication** | Direct and multidirectional | Top-down and formal |
| **Structure** | Fewer layers | Complex hierarchy |
| **Collaboration** | Spontaneous | Planned |

To adopt this approach, it is essential to reduce hierarchical levels, clarify roles, and use collaborative tools like [Trello](https://trello.com/) or [Rolebase](https://rolebase.io/). The results? A significant boost in both productivity and employee satisfaction.

## The Fundamentals of Horizontal Management

### Core Concepts

Horizontal management reduces hierarchical layers by transferring decision-making power to autonomous teams. This gives employees significant freedom to make decisions without needing systematic approval from a superior. This model emphasizes individual responsibility and direct collaboration.

Let's now look at how this model differs from other management styles.

### Comparing Management Styles

The differences between horizontal and vertical management are especially visible across several criteria:

| Criterion | Horizontal Management | Vertical Management |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Structure | A single hierarchical level | Multiple hierarchical levels |
| Approval process | Simple or nonexistent | Complex and structured |
| Distribution of responsibilities | Flexible and shared | Fixed and hierarchical |
| Team coordination | Self-managed | Supervised by the hierarchy |

A notable example is Valve Corporation, a video game company that operates without a traditional hierarchy. Employees choose their own projects and collaborate freely, demonstrating the possibilities offered by this model.

This comparison highlights the strengths and potential limitations of this approach.

### Benefits and Limitations

Horizontal management offers several advantages:

- **Simplified communication**: Exchanges are direct, without going through multiple hierarchical levels.

- **Faster decisions**: Teams can act without waiting for complex approvals.

- **Increased engagement**: Employees feel more involved and responsible.

- **Fosters innovation**: Ideas flow freely, stimulating creativity.

- **Organizational flexibility**: Teams adapt more easily to change.

However, it also comes with challenges:

- **Unclear roles**: The absence of a strict hierarchy can lead to ambiguity.

- **Difficult alignment**: Teams may struggle to coordinate their objectives.

- **Complex management of large organizations**: Overseeing a large number of employees remains a challenge.

To better structure this type of management, tools like Rolebase help organizations maintain effective governance while enabling smooth coordination between teams.

These elements provide a clearer understanding of the impact this model has on team productivity.

## Effects on Team Productivity

### Team Communication

Research indicates that employees spend 28% of their time on emails and 83% in meetings. Horizontal management helps make better use of this time by encouraging direct and more effective exchanges. This clearly demonstrates the practical benefits of this approach.

Take Google as an example. With their open-door policy and TGIF meetings, employees can raise concerns directly with leadership. This type of communication fosters a transparent environment, encourages innovation, and accelerates decision-making.

### Decision Speed and Independence

Direct dialogue between colleagues also shortens decision timelines. Morning Star, a food processing company, illustrates this approach well. Without traditional managers, employees make their own commitments and hold each other accountable. This eliminates unnecessary approval steps, making decisions faster and more autonomous.

### Employee Satisfaction

Autonomy in decision-making has a direct impact on job satisfaction. A study reveals that horizontal communication improves employee performance by 64.9%. Several factors explain this connection:

| Factor | Impact on Satisfaction |
| --- | --- |
| Decision-making autonomy | Strengthens the sense of responsibility |
| Direct communication | Clarifies objectives |
| Cross-team collaboration | Promotes professional growth |
| Recognition of contributions | Increases motivation |

W.L. Gore & Associates perfectly illustrates these benefits. Their "lattice" structure, without a strict hierarchy, allows leaders to act as mentors rather than traditional managers.

## Implementation Steps

### Reducing Hierarchical Levels

To transition to horizontal management, it is important to simplify hierarchical levels. Here are some concrete steps:

- Conduct audits to identify and remove unnecessary structures

- Train teams on the new processes

- Organize collaborative workshops

- Establish clear and measurable indicators

These measures help shorten decision-making timelines while making the organization more responsive. Once this foundation is in place, it is essential to strengthen leadership within teams.

### Building Team Leadership

The example of [Vagas](https://www.vagas.com.br/), an e-recruitment company, illustrates how to structure leadership to foster autonomy. [Vagas](https://www.vagas.com.br/) divided its 150 employees into 30 self-managed teams. The founder of [Vagas](https://www.vagas.com.br/) emphasizes the importance of focusing on processes to achieve desired results naturally.

| Phase | Key Actions | Objectives |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Initial | Training on decision-making mechanisms | Encourage progressive autonomy |
| Development | Role rotation | Promote skill sharing |
| Consolidation | Regular structured meetings | Maintain an aligned collective vision |
| Maturity | Personalized coaching | Strengthen leadership skills |

## Tracking Progress

Tracking progress is essential to maintaining the effectiveness of horizontal management.

### Performance Indicators

To evaluate the effectiveness of horizontal management, use measurable indicators. Here are some important metrics:

| Category | Indicator | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Productivity** | Output per unit of time | Number of features delivered per sprint |
| **Quality** | Satisfaction rate | Customer feedback and error rates |
| **Engagement** | Employee satisfaction | Quarterly survey results |
| **Efficiency** | Completion time | Average time to finish projects |
| **Financial performance** | Revenue per employee | Total revenue divided by the number of employees |

### Team Evaluation Methods

Regular team evaluations help identify areas for improvement. Here are some practical tools:

- **Team barometer**: Measures trust, collaboration, and the quality of exchanges.

- **360° reviews**: Provide a comprehensive perspective on individual performance.

- **Anonymous surveys**: Collect honest feedback on how the team functions.

These tools offer valuable insights for adjusting practices and strengthening teams.

### Leveraging Data for Improvement

Data analysis is a powerful lever for continuously improving performance. Data-driven organizations are 19 times more profitable than those relying solely on intuition. Here are some steps to follow:

- Set up a regular feedback system.

- Use visualization tools to interpret data easily.

- Adjust responsibilities based on each member's skills.

- Refine strategies based on observed team dynamics.

## Common Problems and Solutions

### Responsibilities and Decisions

In horizontal management, clarifying responsibilities is essential. American companies lose approximately $359 billion each year due to unresolved conflicts. To limit these losses, here are some key measures:

- **Define roles precisely**: Use tools like Rolebase to map out responsibilities.

- **Document decision-making processes**: Make sure everyone knows how decisions are made.

- **Establish performance indicators**: Set individual objectives to measure each person's contributions.

A clear structure allows teams to work effectively while preserving their autonomy. Once responsibilities are defined, it is crucial that the entire team shares a common vision.

### Team Alignment

Once roles are clarified, team alignment becomes a key factor for ensuring smooth execution. In fact, 75% of employees want their managers to define more precise objectives.

Here are some strategies to strengthen alignment:

| Aspect | Action | Result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Communication** | Organize weekly sync meetings | Better understanding of objectives |
| **Transparency** | Implement shared dashboards | Increased visibility on progress |
| **Collaboration** | Schedule collective work sessions | Stronger engagement |

To maintain this alignment, it is equally important to anticipate conflicts and manage them effectively.

### Conflict Management

In horizontal management, adopting a proactive approach to conflicts is crucial. Initiating a difficult discussion can cost an organization up to $7,500 and more than seven working days.

Here are three steps to manage conflicts effectively:

1. **Prevention**: Create open communication channels and train teams in nonviolent communication.

2. **Resolution**: Use professional mediation techniques to settle disputes.

3. **Follow-up**: Implement a regular feedback system to identify and defuse tensions early.

It is worth noting that 53% of employees prefer to avoid toxic situations rather than resolve them.
