**Looking to improve collaboration in your organization?** Here are 10 essential practices for successful collaborative governance. They help strengthen efficiency, inclusivity, and transparency in decision-making.

### Summary of the 10 Practices:

1. **Define clear objectives**: Use the SMART method to set precise and measurable goals.

2. **Map out roles**: Clarify responsibilities with tools like the RACI matrix.

3. **Optimize communication**: Adopt suitable tools ([Slack](https://slack.com/), [Talkspirit](https://en.talkspirit.com/)) and clear protocols.

4. **Structure collective decision-making**: Use the DARCI model for more effective choices.

5. **Connect teams across departments**: Create forums and encourage cross-functional exchanges.

6. **Track results**: Measure performance with KPIs and tools like [Monday.com](https://monday.com/).

7. **Establish collective learning**: Encourage knowledge sharing through wikis or collaborative platforms.

8. **Include all members**: Ensure a space for everyone to express themselves, with tools like [Klaxoon](https://klaxoon.com/).

9. **Manage disagreements effectively**: Plan resolution strategies and structured frameworks.

10. **Review and adjust your methods**: Regularly evaluate your practices and adjust them based on results.

These practices, combined with the right tools and clear communication, can transform your organization into a high-performing collaborative space.

## 1. Define Clear Team Objectives

Setting precise objectives is essential for successful collaboration. A recent study shows that companies aligned on their goals experience 58% faster revenue growth and are 72% more profitable than their competitors.

To develop effective objectives, the SMART method is an excellent guide:

- **Specific**: clearly describe what needs to be achieved.

- **Measurable**: include concrete indicators to evaluate progress.

- **Achievable**: aim for realistic and attainable goals.

- **Relevant**: ensure the objectives support the overall strategy.

- **Time-bound**: set precise deadlines.

Only 40% of employees know their company's objectives. To address this, actively involve your teams in defining them.

> "Team members support what they help to build, including the goals they have agreed to be aligned to." - Ronald Rodriguez, Ph.D., PCC

### How to ensure effective team objectives?

**1. Ensure strategic alignment**

Team objectives must be in line with the organization's overall mission. Schedule monthly meetings to verify this alignment and adjust as needed.

**2. Implement transparent tracking**

Use performance indicators (KPIs) accessible to everyone and collaborative tools to monitor progress in real time.

**3. Maintain regular communication**

Plan frequent exchanges to keep everyone on the same page:

- Weekly meetings for status updates.

- Monthly check-ins to evaluate progress.

- Quarterly reviews to adjust priorities.

> "Your message must be clear, concise, concrete, correct, coherent, complete, and courteous to be heard, understood, and internalized. In a word: effective!"

When objectives and responsibilities are well defined, team engagement increases by 2.8x. Clear objectives are therefore the key to smooth and productive collaboration across the entire organization.

## 2. Map Out Team Roles

Well-defined roles help avoid overlap and improve coordination. Once team objectives are established, it is crucial to clarify each person's responsibilities to prevent confusion.

### Using the RACI Matrix

The RACI matrix is a practical tool for assigning and visualizing roles clearly:

| Role | Description | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Responsible | Person(s) who perform(s) the task | Project manager |
| Accountable | Final decision-maker | Department director |
| Consulted | Expert(s) to consult | Technical specialists |
| Informed | Person(s) to keep informed | Stakeholders |

### Steps for Effective Role Mapping

Here are the key steps to succeed in this process:

1. **Identify Essential Tasks**

  List the main activities by reviewing past projects to identify potential sources of friction.

2. **Define Responsibilities**

  Assign each task based on team members' skills and aspirations.

  The [Asana](https://asana.com/) team explains:

> "When roles and responsibilities are clearly defined, each team member understands their specific role, their job description, and what is expected of them. Without well-defined roles, teams can face communication problems, lack of direction, and suboptimal performance."

3. **Establish Decision-Making Mechanisms**

  Set up clear governance to define authority levels, validation processes, and communication channels.

> "To empower community members in decision-making, a collaborative governance structure must include robust mechanisms for transparency, oversight, and accountability."

### Tools and Resources

Platforms like [Rolebase](https://rolebase.io/) offer useful solutions for structuring roles, including:

- Interactive org charts

- Real-time role management

- Task tracking

- Meeting facilitation tools

To keep this mapping relevant, schedule quarterly reviews and encourage feedback. This allows you to quickly adjust roles and improve collaboration within the team. A clear structure is an asset for getting the most out of collective work tools.

## 4. Create Collective Decision-Making Processes

Once roles are well defined, it is time to make your decision-making processes smoother and more effective. Collective decision-making plays a central role in successful collaborative governance.

### DARCI Model for Structuring Collective Decisions

Here is how the DARCI model can help you organize your decisions:

| Role | Description | Responsibility |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Decider | Makes the final decision | Validates the decision |
| Accountable | Oversees follow-up | Coordinates implementation |
| Consulted | Shares expertise | Advises without deciding |
| Informed | Stays in the loop | Receives updates |

Inspired by the RACI model, DARCI is particularly useful for structuring decisions based on team size and needs.

### Decision Methods Based on Team Size

**Small teams (fewer than 15 people)**:

- Encourage quick, well-organized discussions.

- Systematically document decisions made.

**Larger teams (up to 50 people)**:

- Use tools like Klaxoon to organize votes.

- Set clear time limits for discussions.

- Maintain detailed documentation for each decision.

### Example: The Sacramento Water Forum

A concrete example of a successful collective decision-making process:

- Participation of all stakeholders involved.

- Operating rules established collectively.

- A framework built on mutual understanding.

- Transparent sharing of key information.

### Tools for Facilitating Collective Decision-Making

Here are some techniques and their advantages:

| Technique | Use | Advantages |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Brainstorming | Generate ideas | Encourages broad participation. |
| Delphi Method | Reduce divergences | Helps consolidate ideas. |
| Dialectical Inquiry | Compare two options | Enables in-depth analysis. |
| Consensus Mapping | Handle complex decisions | Provides a clear overview. |

For optimal follow-up, tools like Rolebase can facilitate your meetings and ensure complete traceability of decisions made. Make sure each participant understands their role and the process steps to guarantee effective collaboration.

## 9. Manage Disagreements Effectively

Handling disagreements constructively is essential for maintaining collaborative governance. Unresolved conflicts can cost over €7,500 and stall an organization for more than seven days.

### Understanding the Impact of Conflicts

Conflicts can have a major impact: some managers spend up to 20% of their time on them. Identifying the type of conflict and adopting the appropriate strategy is crucial.

| Type of Conflict | Impact on the Organization | Resolution Strategy |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Interpersonal conflict | Deterioration of social climate | Mediation and open dialogue |
| Task-related conflict | Decreased productivity | Clarification of objectives |
| Process conflict | Operational inefficiency | Procedure review |

### Anticipate to Reduce Conflicts

Acting proactively can reduce conflict escalation by 40%. Here are some measures to implement:

- **Develop clear guidelines** for handling disagreements.

- **Organize training sessions** in conflict resolution.

- **Establish smooth communication channels**.

- **Set up regular feedback mechanisms**.

### Effective Resolution Approaches

A collaborative approach based on active listening can transform conflicts into opportunities. This method increases misunderstanding resolution by 40%. The goal is to find solutions where all parties come out ahead.

### Structured Framework for Resolution

To structure these efforts, adopt a framework that:

1. Allows each person to express their interests and concerns.

2. Encourages constructive and professional dialogue.

3. Integrates regular feedback.

4. Ensures follow-up on agreements and solutions.

This framework fosters better cohesion and prepares teams to handle disagreements more effectively.

### Training Teams in Key Skills

Strengthen your teams with specific skills such as:

- **Constructive negotiation** to reach balanced agreements.

- **Mediation** to defuse tensions.

- **Nonviolent communication** to prevent misunderstandings.

- **Emotion management** to maintain a calm climate.

Investing in these areas improves collaboration and supports more effective and harmonious governance within the organization.
