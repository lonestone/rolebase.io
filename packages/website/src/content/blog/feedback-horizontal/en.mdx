### Regular Feedback Sessions

To maximize the impact of feedback, it is essential to structure each session. This starts with careful preparation and continues with a clear framework for exchanges.

**How to properly prepare a feedback session?**

- Send an agenda in advance so everyone can prepare.

- Set precise objectives to guide the discussion.

A good balance is also crucial: a ratio of 5 positive comments for every 1 constructive remark is recommended. The example of W.L. Gore & Associates is telling: their "lattice" organizational structure encourages direct communication between colleagues for smoother decision-making.

### How can you train teams to give and receive constructive feedback?

## Training Teams to Exchange Constructive Feedback

To help your teams master the art of constructive feedback, start by explaining the importance of **properly preparing their input**. This means relying on specific facts, clearly defining the objective of the feedback, and choosing an appropriate time and place for the discussion. Encourage them to use language focused on actions rather than individuals. For example, favor phrases like *"I notice that..."* rather than *"You always..."*. This approach reduces the risk of misunderstandings and promotes respectful communication.

Receiving feedback also requires an **open and receptive attitude**. Invite team members to listen carefully, without interrupting, and to reflect on the comments received. It helps to view feedback as learning opportunities rather than personal criticism. A humble and collaborative posture helps build a climate of trust, essential for encouraging continuous improvement. Over time and with practice, these exchanges become a true driver of progress, both for individuals and for the team as a whole.
