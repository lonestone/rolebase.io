## What is Rolebase?

Rolebase is an open-source SaaS platform designed for organizations that practice **Holacracy**, **Sociocracy**, and other **self-governance models**. It provides a unified workspace where teams can define their structure, run meetings, manage tasks, hold discussions, and record decisions, all aligned with the principles of distributed authority.

Whether you are a [liberated company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberated_company), a cooperative, or any team experimenting with decentralized management, Rolebase gives you the tools to make self-governance practical and transparent.

## Who is it for?

- **Organizations practicing Holacracy** can define roles (which act as circles when they contain sub-roles), purpose, domains, and accountabilities.
- **Sociocracy teams** can structure consent-based decision-making with nested roles.
- **Liberated companies** can give every team member clarity on responsibilities without top-down hierarchy.
- **Any team exploring self-management** can start small and evolve their governance model over time.

## Core Concepts

Rolebase is built around a few key ideas:

### Organization
The top-level container. Everything (roles, members, meetings) lives inside an organization. Each organization has its own settings, subscription, and member list.

### Roles (and Circles)
In Rolebase, the concepts of circle and role are **unified into a single entity: the role**. A role can represent an individual responsibility, or it can contain other roles, in which case it acts as what Holacracy or Sociocracy would call a "circle". Each role has a purpose, domain, and accountabilities. You can use the terms "circle" and "role" interchangeably. [Learn more about Roles](/en/docs/circles-and-roles).

### Members
People in your organization. Each member can hold one or more roles. Members are invited via email and assigned an organization-level role (Owner, Admin, Member, or Readonly). [Learn more about Members](/en/docs/members).

### Meetings
Structured meetings with defined steps including tours, checklists, indicators, tasks, and threads review. Meetings can follow templates and recur on a schedule. [Learn more about Meetings](/en/docs/meetings).

### Tasks
Actionable items assigned to roles and members with statuses, due dates, and two view modes: kanban board and list. [Learn more about Tasks](/en/docs/tasks).

### Threads
Asynchronous discussion spaces within roles. Threads support messaging, polls, and reactions for collaborative decision-making. [Learn more about Threads](/en/docs/threads).

### Decisions
Formal records of governance changes and agreements made within roles. [Learn more about Decisions](/en/docs/decisions).
