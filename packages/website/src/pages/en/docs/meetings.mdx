## Overview

Meetings in Rolebase are structured, step-based sessions designed for self-governed teams. They follow a defined flow that keeps discussions focused and productive, inspired by Holacratic governance and tactical meetings.

Each meeting belongs to a role and follows a sequence of steps that you can customize.

## Meeting Lifecycle

### 1. Create

Create a meeting from a role's meeting section. You can start from scratch or use a **meeting template**. Set the title, date/time, and participants.

### 2. Start

When it is time, the facilitator starts the meeting. This transitions the meeting from a scheduled state to an active session.

### 3. Run Steps

The meeting progresses through a series of steps. The facilitator advances through each step while participants contribute.

### 4. End

When all steps are complete, the facilitator ends the meeting. A summary of the meeting is generated and stored.

## Meeting Steps

Each meeting is composed of ordered steps. The available step types are:

### Tour

An opening round where each participant shares a brief check-in. This can be used for an icebreaker, a personal update, or setting intentions for the meeting.

### Threads

Review and discuss active [threads](/en/docs/threads) in the role. Participants can raise topics, update statuses, and make decisions on ongoing discussions.

### Checklist

Go through the role's recurring checklist items. Each member confirms whether they completed their items since the last meeting. This provides accountability and transparency.

### Indicators

Review the role's key metrics and indicators. Members report on numbers and trends, giving the team visibility into operational health.

### Tasks

Review [tasks](/en/docs/tasks) assigned to role members. Update statuses, discuss blockers, and create new tasks as needed.

<Callout type="tip" title="Step Order">
  The default order (Tour, Checklist, Indicators, Tasks, Threads) follows the Holacratic tactical meeting format, but you can reorder, add, or remove steps to fit your team's needs.
</Callout>

## Meeting Templates

Templates define a reusable set of steps for meetings. Instead of configuring steps each time, create a template and apply it when scheduling new meetings.

Common templates:

- **Tactical Meeting** includes Tour, Checklist, Indicators, Tasks, Threads.
- **Governance Meeting** includes Tour, Threads (focused on governance proposals).
- **Quick Sync** includes Tour, Tasks.

Templates are defined at the role level and can be reused for every meeting of that role.

## Recurring Meetings

Meetings can be configured to recur on a schedule using **recurrence rules** (rrule format). For example:

- Every Monday at 10:00 AM
- First Wednesday of every month
- Every two weeks on Friday

When a recurring meeting is set up, future occurrences are automatically created according to the schedule.

## Calendar Sync

You can connect your calendar (Google Calendar, Office 365) to Rolebase so that meetings automatically appear in your personal calendar. When a meeting is created, updated, or deleted, the change is reflected in your connected calendar.

To set up calendar sync, see [Apps & Integrations](/en/docs/apps-integrations).

## iCal Export

You can export your meetings as an iCal feed to view them in any calendar app:

1. Go to the **Meetings** page of your role.
2. Open the settings menu (top right).
3. Select **Export (iCal)**.
4. Copy the iCal URL displayed in the modal.
5. Paste it into your calendar app as a subscription (Google Calendar, Outlook, Apple Calendar all support this).

The feed includes all meetings for your organization and updates automatically. Synchronization is one-way and may take a few minutes. For fast two-way synchronization, connect a calendar directly via [Apps & Integrations](/en/docs/apps-integrations).

## Participants

### Scope

You can define who is invited to the meeting:

- **All role members** means every member of the role is a participant.
- **Specific members** lets you hand-pick participants from the role.

### Attendees Tracking

During and after the meeting, track attendance with three statuses:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Present** | The member attended the meeting |
| **Absent** | The member did not attend |
| **Maybe** | The member has not confirmed attendance |

Attendance records are saved with the meeting for historical reference.

## Video Conferencing

Rolebase integrates with video conferencing tools. When starting a meeting, you can launch a video call directly. The video link is shared with all participants.

## Private Meetings

Meetings can be marked as **private**. Private meetings are only visible to their participants and are hidden from other role members. This is useful for sensitive discussions like performance reviews or personnel matters.

## Meeting Summary

When a meeting ends, Rolebase generates a summary that includes:

- Date, time, and duration
- Attendees and their status
- Notes and outcomes from each step
- Tasks created or updated during the meeting
- Thread decisions made

The summary serves as an official record of the meeting and is accessible from the role's meeting history.

<Callout type="tip" title="AI Summary">
  Meeting summaries can be auto-generated, providing a concise recap of discussions and decisions for members who were absent.
</Callout>
