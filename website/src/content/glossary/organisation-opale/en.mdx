A teal organization, based on the principles outlined in [Frederic Laloux](/en/glossary/frederic-laloux)'s book "Reinventing Organizations," is a self-organized model that emphasizes alignment with values, individual responsibility, and collaboration within the organization. It is often considered the most evolved type of organization according to Laloux's vision.

The concept of a teal organization is strongly influenced by the deep values and beliefs of its members. It is a model that seeks to create an environment where everyone is encouraged to fully express their creativity and potential, and to actively contribute to achieving the company's goals.

One of the key characteristics of a teal organization is self-organization, where teams and individuals are autonomous in making decisions about their work. This fosters individual responsibility and the ability to respond quickly to challenges and opportunities.

Collaboration is also at the heart of a teal organization. Members work closely and transparently together, sharing their knowledge and skills to achieve common goals. Traditional hierarchies are often reduced or eliminated in favor of a more egalitarian approach.

It is important to note that implementing a teal organization can be complex and requires strong commitment from all members. It relies on principles of mutual trust, transparency, and personal responsibility.

In summary, a teal organization is a self-organized model that emphasizes value alignment, individual responsibility, and collaboration within the organization. It represents an evolutionary vision of business management that seeks to unleash the creative and human potential of its members, in line with Frederic Laloux's philosophy as described in his book "Reinventing Organizations." Its implementation can be challenging and requires significant commitment to succeed.
