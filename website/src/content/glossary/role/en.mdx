"Roles," in the context of Role Based Management (RBM), are clearly defined functions within an organization, and each role is responsible for specific tasks. This approach encourages autonomy and innovation by allowing each organization member to contribute in an agile way to the common objective. Roles evolve in response to needs, promoting a dynamic distribution of work rather than a fixed hierarchy.

Each role in the organization has clearly defined responsibilities, which provides an overview of the tasks and activities that must be accomplished to achieve objectives. Organization members are assigned to roles based on their skills and capabilities, which promotes their involvement and active contribution.

The role-based approach encourages autonomy, as each member is responsible for their specific area of work. This allows for decentralized decision-making and enables members to take initiative to accomplish their tasks effectively.

Furthermore, roles are designed to be adaptable. They evolve according to the organization's needs, promoting a dynamic distribution of work in response to internal and external changes. This flexibility allows the organization to quickly adjust to challenges and opportunities that arise.

In summary, roles in RBM are clearly defined functions that encourage autonomy, innovation, and active contribution from organization members. They adapt in response to needs, promoting a dynamic distribution of work and an agile approach to achieving the organization's common objectives.
