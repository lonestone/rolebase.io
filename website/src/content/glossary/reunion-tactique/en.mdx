A "tactical meeting" is an operational meeting whose primary objective is to coordinate actions and optimize processes in order to achieve organizational goals. This meeting is essential for ensuring effective coordination of activities and resources within the organization.

The term "tactical" suggests a pragmatic, execution-oriented approach. Tactical meetings typically focus on specific topics related to daily operations and aim to solve concrete problems, make immediate decisions, and ensure that activities are aligned with the organization's strategic objectives.

The agenda of a tactical meeting is usually established in advance to ensure that discussion points are clearly defined and that the meeting stays focused on operational priorities. Participants in these meetings are often team members responsible for implementing ongoing initiatives and projects.

Tactical meetings are an essential element of operational management and help track progress, identify obstacles, and take corrective action when necessary. They also foster communication and collaboration within the team, contributing to the overall effectiveness of the organization.

In summary, a "tactical meeting" is an operational meeting aimed at coordinating actions and optimizing processes to achieve organizational goals. It is characterized by its execution-oriented focus, its pre-established agenda, and its central role in the day-to-day management of the organization's activities.
