"Indicators," in the context of management and performance measurement, are specific metrics used to evaluate the progress of a role, a circle, or a member within an organization. These metrics are designed to provide quantitative or qualitative data that measure and monitor various aspects of organizational activity, from operational effectiveness to strategic outcomes.

Indicators are crucial for guiding an organization toward achieving its goals. They are generally aligned with the organization's priorities and strategies, and they measure performance against those objectives. Indicators can cover a wide range of areas, such as productivity, quality, profitability, customer satisfaction, regulatory compliance, environmental sustainability, and more.

Using indicators enables informed decision-making, helps determine where adjustments or improvements are needed, and identifies long-term trends in the organization's performance. They play an essential role in performance management, strategic planning, feedback, and continuous improvement.

Indicators can be expressed through dashboards, charts, or reports, and regular monitoring allows leaders and managers to stay informed about the health and direction of the organization.

In summary, "indicators" are metrics used to evaluate the performance of a role, a circle, or a member within an organization. They are essential for guiding the organization toward its strategic goals, making informed decisions, and promoting continuous improvement across various areas of activity.
