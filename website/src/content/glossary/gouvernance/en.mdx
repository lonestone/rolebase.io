Governance refers to the way authority is distributed within an organization's decision-making structures, with the goal of directing and effectively coordinating all of its activities. This concept encompasses the processes, rules, responsibilities, and decision-making mechanisms that allow an organization to function in an orderly and coherent manner.

Governance can take many forms depending on the size and nature of the organization. It may include governing bodies such as boards of directors, executive committees, or general assemblies, as well as decision-making protocols, policies, and internal procedures.

Governance plays an essential role in the organization's strategic direction, performance monitoring, risk management, and compliance with applicable regulations and standards. It also helps establish a framework of responsibility, transparency, and accountability within the organization.

In a corporate context, corporate governance specifically refers to the way a company is directed and controlled, particularly by its shareholders, board of directors, and management. Good corporate governance is often considered essential to ensuring the company's long-term viability, building investor confidence, and maintaining ethical business practices.

Governance is a central element in the management of public, private, and nonprofit organizations, and it often evolves to meet the changing needs of the organization and its environment.

The impact of horizontal management approaches, such as Holacracy or teal organizations, on organizational governance is significant. These approaches emphasize distributing authority more equitably, encouraging the active participation of all organization members in decision-making. In these models, traditional hierarchical structures are often replaced by autonomous circles or cross-functional teams that make collective decisions.

This has a direct impact on governance, as decisions are no longer made solely by a small group of leaders but by a larger number of people within the organization. This promotes transparency, shared responsibility, and a better understanding of issues at all levels of the organization.

However, these horizontal management approaches often require a cultural transformation and an adjustment of traditional governance mechanisms to function effectively. They may also be better suited to certain organizations or industries than others, depending on the nature of their activities and governance needs. Overall, these approaches broaden thinking about governance by recognizing that authority and decision-making can be distributed in a more democratic and participatory way, which can have a positive impact on the organization's culture and performance.

In summary, "governance" involves organizing the distribution of authority within an organization through decision-making structures, processes, and mechanisms. It aims to guide the organization, ensure transparency and accountability, and enable informed decision-making to achieve its objectives and meet the expectations of its stakeholders.
