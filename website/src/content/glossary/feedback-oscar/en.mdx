The OSCAR feedback method is a powerful communication tool for strengthening constructive feedback within a team or organization. By focusing on five key aspects, it provides a structured approach to conveying information and ensures that the message is understood objectively rather than emotionally.

**OS - Specific Observation of facts**

First, the Specific Observation of facts (OS) ensures that feedback is based on concrete, verifiable elements, eliminating subjective interpretations that can lead to misunderstandings. This first step encourages communication grounded in tangible information, reducing potential ambiguity.

**C - Consequence**

Next, the Consequence (C) highlights the impact of the observed behavior or situation. It allows the feedback giver to share their feelings and impressions while maintaining a focus on the facts. This dimension of OSCAR feedback promotes emotionally intelligent communication, helping the recipient fully understand the repercussions of their actions.

**A - Actionability**

Actionability (A) is an essential element of the OSCAR method, as it focuses on the concrete steps to take in order to resolve or improve the situation. By providing constructive suggestions, it steers the conversation toward solutions rather than dwelling on problems.

**R - Expected Results**

Finally, the Expected Results (R) project a positive future by describing the desired changes following the recommended action. This step allows the recipient to visualize the potential benefits of an adjustment or behavioral change.

By using the OSCAR method, feedback becomes more constructive and less likely to trigger defensive reactions. It fosters a climate of trust and collaboration within the team, encouraging problem-solving and continuous improvement.

To successfully put the OSCAR feedback method into practice, it is essential to master it and deploy it consistently across the organization. This requires awareness and training for team members to ensure it is used effectively and with care. Well-formulated feedback using the OSCAR method can contribute to healthier professional relationships and an organizational culture focused on learning and continuous improvement.
