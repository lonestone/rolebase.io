"Poka-Yoke" is a term rooted in the context of error prevention. It refers to the implementation of devices or mechanisms designed to make errors impossible or extremely difficult to commit. This approach aims to minimize the risk of human error by designing systems, processes, or devices that incorporate checks or safety mechanisms to prevent mistakes from occurring.

The goal of "Poka-Yoke" is to create an environment where errors are proactively anticipated and avoided through preventive design. This can include measures such as simplifying procedures, adding automatic checks, using visual guides, or training employees to avoid common mistakes.

The "Poka-Yoke" approach is inspired by a philosophy of prevention rather than correction, recognizing that preventing errors is often more effective and economical than fixing problems after they occur. It is particularly important in fields where errors can have serious consequences, such as in the medical, aerospace, and nuclear industries.

For example, a "Poka-Yoke" could be a keyed connector on an electrical plug that prevents plugging a device in the wrong way.

In summary, "Poka-Yoke" represents a proactive approach to error prevention by implementing devices that make errors impossible or extremely difficult to commit. It aims to minimize the risk of human error by designing systems and processes that are resilient to mistakes.
