Change management is a process aimed at guiding an organized transition to bring about smooth adaptations within the organization. Its primary objective is to enable effective organizational evolution by managing the human, organizational, and cultural aspects related to change.

This process recognizes that change, whether related to new technologies, structural reorganization, or policy shifts, can generate resistance and concerns within the organization. Change management seeks to reduce this resistance by implementing strategies and measures to facilitate the transition.

Change management can involve various activities, such as proactive communication, stakeholder engagement, training and skills development, managing employee emotions, and creating a corporate culture that embraces change. It relies on careful planning and a thorough understanding of the needs and concerns of those affected by the change.

In a constantly evolving world, change management has become an essential discipline for organizations seeking to adapt, innovate, and remain competitive. It aims to minimize disruptions and reluctance while maximizing employee acceptance and buy-in for new initiatives.

In summary, "change management" is a process aimed at facilitating organizational transition by managing the human, cultural, and organizational aspects related to change. It relies on communication, stakeholder engagement, and creating an environment that embraces change to ensure effective organizational evolution in a constantly evolving world.
