The term "agile" is a qualifier used to describe a process, practice, or any element that aligns with the principles and best practices of [agile methodologies](/en/glossary/methodes-agiles). Agility is a concept often associated with project management and software development methodologies that emphasize flexibility, collaboration, communication, and adaptability.

Agility is built on a set of key values and principles, such as customer satisfaction, collaboration among individuals and with clients, responsiveness to change, frequent delivery of functional products, and simplicity. Agile methodologies, like Scrum or Kanban, are designed to enable teams to work iteratively and incrementally, quickly adapting to feedback and changes in customer needs.

The term "agile" is also used more broadly to describe a corporate culture or management approach that promotes flexibility, innovation, and responsiveness. "Agile" companies are often characterized by their ability to quickly adapt to market changes and capitalize on emerging opportunities.

Agility has become a key concept in the business world, particularly in technology and software development industries. It is often associated with practices such as iterative planning, visual work management, retrospectives, and continuous improvement.

In summary, the term "agile" qualifies something that conforms to the principles and best practices of agile methodologies, which emphasize flexibility, collaboration, communication, and adaptability. Agility is widely used in software development and has become an essential concept for companies seeking to remain responsive and innovative in a constantly evolving market.
