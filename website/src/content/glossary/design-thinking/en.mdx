"Design thinking" is a creative, user-centered approach that aims to foster innovation and problem-solving. This working method is based on the idea that to create effective solutions, it is essential to deeply understand the needs and perspectives of end users.

At the heart of design thinking lies a deep empathy for users. Teams that use this approach seek to immerse themselves in the user experience, identify their challenges and aspirations, and see the world through their eyes. This deep understanding is essential for generating ideas and solutions that truly address users' real needs.

The design thinking process is often structured in several stages, such as problem definition, research and observation, idea generation, prototyping, and testing. It encourages creativity, interdisciplinary collaboration, and rapid experimentation to find innovative solutions.

Design thinking is widely used across many fields, including product design, services, social innovation, and solving complex problems. It is aimed at companies seeking to improve their products or services by placing the end user at the center of the design process.

In summary, "design thinking" is a user-centered approach that encourages innovation and problem-solving based on a deep understanding of users' needs and perspectives. This methodology fosters creativity, interdisciplinary collaboration, and rapid experimentation to develop effective, user-centered solutions across various fields, from product design to services and social innovation.
