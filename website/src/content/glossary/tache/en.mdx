"Tasks," in the context of Role Based Management (RBM), are specific and measurable work items that stem from the roles defined within the organization's circles. They are transparently assigned to competent members, which promotes individual accountability and contributes to achieving organizational objectives.

Tasks are the concrete and precise actions required to carry out the responsibilities and functions of each role within the organization. They are typically defined in relation to the objectives and activities of the corresponding circle. This clear task definition helps determine who is responsible for what, strengthening individual accountability among organization members.

Transparent task assignment also helps ensure that members clearly understand what is expected of them and what their specific responsibilities are within the team or circle. This facilitates coordination and collaboration within the organization, as everyone knows what they need to accomplish to contribute to shared goals.

Tasks can vary in size and complexity, ranging from daily actions to longer-term projects. They are generally aligned with the organization's mission and priorities, making them essential for achieving overall objectives.

In summary, tasks in RBM are specific and measurable work items that stem from defined roles within the organization's circles. They are transparently assigned to competent members, thereby strengthening individual accountability and contributing to achieving the organization's objectives.
